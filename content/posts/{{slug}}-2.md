Located about an hour west of Chicago is the lonesome city of Dekalb, Illinois.  I am familiar with the city only by its famous Dekalb Oasis.  The Oasis is home to greats such as multiple claw machines, a perpetually closed Popeyes, and maybe the grossist bathroom in Illinois.  Dekalb is also home to Northern Illinois University, where my friend Mike and I spent a windy afternoon watching Northern Illinois take on MAC rival Ball State.  

Prior to the match-up Mike and I stopped in neighboring Sycamore, Illinois as while doing research I came across a behemoth of a pork tenderloin sandwich.  The Dirty Bird restaurant has a pork tenderloin sandwich that is the size of a lunchroom tray that is comically put between a single hamburger bun and a few pickles.  While entering the restaurant I gestured to Mike, a man eating the sandwich we planned on ordering and asked the fella if it was any good.

Eric: “Any good?”

Man: “Yea real good, been looking forward to it all day

Eric: _polite smile and nod_

Man: “You guys here from the group”

Eric: “Group? Which group”

Man “The pork tenderloin facebook group, that’s how I found this place”

Mike “No, we aren’t in the group”

I did not know there was a facebook group and it did not really hit me what the man had said until after our meal. I asked Mike “Did that guy say he was in a facebook group just for pork tenderloin sandwiches?” We laughed for a bit and continued to the game.

The sandwich was pretty good, if you are ever in the area check them out.
