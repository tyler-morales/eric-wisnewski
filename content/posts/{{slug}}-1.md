The game I attended was an ACC classic of Boston College v. Cal - Berkeley, noon tip-off. 

**Campus: B**

Chestnut Hill is a small village located about eight miles west of Boston that is best known for being the home of Jesuit Catholic college Boston College.   The vibe felt homey, close knit, and a white collar community with homes that all seemed to be historic landmarks.  Windy roads lined with some of the most immaculate brick homes located everywhere. Right in the middle of the village sitting on a steep hill was the campus for Boston College.  All the buildings for the college seemed to be built using a grayish cobblestone material which blended in with the gray skies above.  Beautiful buildings that reminded me of where the movie The Holdovers or Dead Poet’s Society was filmed.  It was silent, I mean almost eerie like walking around the campus as very few students roamed on this early Saturday afternoon.  If it weren’t for the basketball game I do not know if I would have ran into any sort of large congregation of people.  The campus itself is small, taking about 20 minutes in total to walk from end to end.    From what I could tell there was not a central hub for commercial buildings as I am assuming most commerce is conducted in neighboring Newton, Brighton, or the aforementioned Boston.  Chestnut Hill exists purely for the college it seems.  Pretty, but boring.

**Stadium: D**

Boston College plays basketball at the Conte Forum, directly next to the football stadium. Once you walk in you can tell the forum was built with hockey in mind first. The rafters of the building are caked with banners recognizing the success of their hockey program which seems to be the main draw of their college athletics.  It was a fucking sauna in this place, like uncomfortably hot.  I am not one that typically overheats but I was in a world of pain (I was also a tad bit hungover).  The concourse did not feature much natural sunlight as most of it was thick cinder block walls with collegiate hall of farmers plaques decorating the walls.  I am not a sports historian, especially on the collegiate end, so the only people I really recognized were Neugenix-maxxing Doug Flutie and hooper Jared Dudley (2014 Milwaukee Buck super sub).  Entrances to sections were marked by a confusing lettering system and a series of arrows pointing all cardinal directions.  I had to ask three people where my seat was.  The first guy just pointed down the hall, the second literally shrugged and said “I don’t know man”, and finally a third was able to get me to my seat.  The forum has two semi bowl-like sections followed by folding chairs on the baseline ends and two bleachers/chairs seating sections on the sidelines.  On one baseline there was a section that I could not tell if it was box seating or offices for the athletic programs.  Capacity for the arena is about 8,500 people.  According to ESPN, approximately 3,500 people were in attendance for this game.

**Fan Base: F**

One of the most disappointing things a school can do is not having a band.  I look forward to the bands.  They play covers of your mom and dad favorite songs and usually break up the monotony of timeouts/commercial breaks. A good band can turn a shitty experience into a good one real quick. I like the bands.  I need a band.  Boston College did not have a band in attendance on the day that I went to the game.  They may have a band regularly, but on this particular day they did not.  This left us with the same rehashed stadium music which is usually clean versions of Rap Caviar or whatever is playing on FM radio.  

A common trend for sporting events now is hosts of the game. Their goal is engagement from the crowd, which I find rarely ever works.  Engagement from the crowd should come either through college traditions (jump around at Wisconsin) or general interest in the product that they are being presented (quality and competitive basketball).  Entertainment’s equivalent to shaking keys in front of a baby, fleeting and eventually boring.  Boston College did have an in-game host but the guy probably took to the mic maybe five times the entire time.  Why even attempt to have a host if you’re going to be this lazy with it.  

The student base that turned out to the game could have been counted on two hands.  This is not an exaggeration.  I think there were literally 10 students in attendance and most did not show up until the second half.  Now, as I said earlier Boston College is more known for their hockey program.  The men’s hockey team had a game later in the evening so maybe students were waiting to attend that game instead of this one.  Still I cannot speak on what I do not know so the rating for fans is reflective of the effort shown by their student base.  An absolute zero from the students.

![](/images/uploads/IMG_3658.jpeg)

For those who were there it was mostly kids and their families.  A common trend I am noticing on these weekend day games is that a lot of youth basketball teams attend.  The ruckus these kids can cause may add to the volume of the stadium but do not improve the intimidation factor.  A rain of “6-7”’s and screaming directly in my ear was common at this game.  I cannot get overly mad at these kids because I was doing the same shit at their age.  I was an absolute menace as a child when stadiums showed the decibels in an arena.  Chestnut Hill seemed like an area where a lot of families call home, so it is not overly surprising that this many kids were in attendance.

Overall I did not have a good time.  The team is not very good which does not make the experience any better.  At the time of writing Boston College has fired their coach as of this week.  So, maybe things will look-up, but doubtful.  Boston College has no draw for college basketball and seem to put their energy towards their other sporting programs like football and hockey, which is perfectly fine.  I think this is a game that most people can miss and dare I say avoid.  Do not waste your time in Boston by going to a BC basketball game like I did.  As bad as the basketball experience was, the campus may be worth your time to check out as I did enjoy looking around.  However, there’s one large old historic campus located not too far away that is better viewing than this one.
