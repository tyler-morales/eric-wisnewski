I hate Chicago winters.  A constant battle with the elements, shorter days, and a lack of overall energy drapes the city.  The only way to combat this periodic case of winter blues best is to keep busy.  In past years in my earlier years living in Chicago it would be exploring the surrounding area through bars, restaurants, or any other entertainment options with friends.  One constant was always sports.  I am a massive basketball fan, specifically the Chicago Bulls.  However, due to the incompetence of ownership and no clear plan for the future my love of the game has waivered.  I wanted to recapture my love of  watching basketball so I purchased season tickets to DePaul Men’s Basketball along with my dad.  I chose a great year to buy into DePaul as they are having their best season, arguably, since 2007.  Attending every game this year and seeing the organic growth in interest of the program from the city really struck a cord.  As the team got better attendance got better and a sense of community started to take shape.  Along with DePaul’s resurgence of a presence in the Big East, my alumna mater, The University of Iowa, had hired a new coach providing hope that things were finally changing at a stagnant sports program like Iowa’s.  This season has acted as a perfect storm in regaining my interest in college basketball, which is something I have not had in a long time.  With the constant monitoring of [kenpom.com](http://kenpom.com) and ESPN box scores, I had become aware of the existence of the other 363 division one basketball teams in the country.  Then I asked myself “What if I visited them all?”

There are 365 division one programs as of 2025-26 season with 314 unique cities across 49 states (sorry Alaska). Has it been done before?  Has someone visited every division one team and watched a game?  I googled it and the AI did not have a definitive answer.  So, based on this thorough research the answer is no.  If someone is going to achieve this impossible task it is going to have to be me.  Something people compliment me on is my bravery to never quit, so humbly I could not think of literally anyone else that could achieve this feat.  Try and stop me.

![](/images/uploads/PNG%20image.jpeg)

 My favorite thing to do on a road trip is stop off at the colleges on the way to my destinations.  I have no idea why, but I have an infatuation with colleges and their towns.  This “challenge” (fucking hate calling it that) will quench my urge to see the United States and its great educational institutes.  I wanted to create this blog to document my journey so I can look back at it and have a good laugh when I’m older.  When mentioning this idea to my friends and family a lot of people brought up the point that it would take my entire life.  That is kind of the point.  I do not want to finish this anytime soon.  Traveling and meeting those outside your immediate circle leads to constant growth as a person. I have seen this amongst my friends and know it to be true.  Life has become stagnant in recent years, which I am not saying is a bad thing, but stopping any sort of self-growth is poisonous.  Therapy is another option, but like come on. 

Some rules I am setting in place.  I must attend a game at every division one game for at least 90% of the game (up until the last four minute timeout).  I will rank the experience in three categories; stadium, fan base, and campus. All rankings are subjective and come with a large bias, I will admit that now so I do not have to in the future. Below are the questions I will be asking myself while going through my rankings.

*   Stadium:  Is the facility made for basketball?  How are the sight lines? Age of the arena or historic precedence?  Game Day experience? Location of stadium to campus?  As well as anything of prominence that comes to mind (will be disclosed in individual blogs)
    
*   Fan Base:  This is probably the most situational of them all as teams go through program ebbs and flows of national/regional relevance.  Whatever, I cannot control that at time of attendance.  Are there students at the game?  Fan traditions? Marching band? Overall attendance? Are fans engaged in the game?
    
*   Campus:  Aesthetic of the campus? How is the college town?  Is the university a cornerstone of the community?  
    

A lot of these rankings are a gut feeling and really are not that quantitative, but like I said I report on a bias.  These are all my opinions.  

I intend to write a blog shortly after visiting the college, but this is not my full time job and I like to think I have some sort of a life outside of college basketball.  Do not expect a concrete schedule of posting.  It is also just so perfect for me to start a project like this in late February as the season is coming to an end.  However, I fully intend to commit to this cause starting in the 2026-27 season.  With that being said, since thinking of this idea I have been able to visit three new stadiums which I want to have blogs written about in the next coming weeks.
